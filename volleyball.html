Rules & Regulations

1. General Gameplay & Teams

  • Objective: The goal is to score points by sending the ball over the net so it lands within the opponent's court.
  • Teams: Games are played between two teams. The number of players on the court will be decided per match.
  • Scoring: We will use rally scoring. A point is awarded on every rally, regardless of which team served. Sets are typically played to 25 points, and a team must win by at least a two-point margin.

2. Hitting & Attacks (Custom Rules)

  • Consecutive Hits: A single player is allowed to hit the ball twice in a row.
  • Team Hits: There is no limit to the total number of hits a team can make before sending the ball over the net.
  • Legal Contact: Contact with the ball must be a clean, brief hit. Holding, catching, lifting, or throwing the ball is not permitted and will result in a fault.

3. Serving (Custom Rules)

  • Service Style: All serves must be performed underhand. Overhand or jump serves are not allowed.
  • Service Location: The server is permitted to serve from inside the court. The serve must be executed from behind the 3-meter (10-foot) attack line.
  • Valid Serve: The served ball must cross the net and travel over the opponent's 3-meter attack line to be considered a valid serve.
  • Service Rotation: Service must be done by each player in the team in a rotational sequence.
  • Continuous Serving: A player can continue to serve as long as their team keeps winning points. When the serving team loses a rally, the serve switches to the opposing team, and they must rotate to their next server.

4. Fouls & Violations

  • Net Contact: Players are not allowed to touch the net during play. If a player touches the net, their team loses the point.
  • Center Line: A player may step on the center line, but their entire foot cannot cross completely over the line into the opponent's court.
  • Out of Bounds: A ball is considered 'out' if it lands completely outside the boundary lines, touches the ceiling, or hits the antennas on the net. A ball that lands on a boundary line is 'in'.

5. Sportsmanship

  • All players are expected to show good sportsmanship.
  • The referee's calls are final. Please respect their decisions.
  • Let's prioritize safety, fair play, and fun for everyone involved!
