Rules & Regulations

1. The Basics

  • Two teams compete to score more runs than the other.
  • The game is played in innings, where each team takes a turn to bat and bowl.
  • The batting team tries to score runs, while the bowling team tries to get the batters "out."

2. Scoring Runs

  • Runs are scored by hitting the ball and running between two sets of wickets.
  • If the ball is hit to the boundary, it's an automatic 4 runs (if it bounces) or 6 runs (if it doesn't).

3. Getting Out

  • Bowled: The bowler hits the wickets with the ball.
  • Caught: A fielder catches the ball after it's been hit by the batter, but before it bounces.
  • Run Out: A fielder hits the wickets with the ball while the batter is running between them.
